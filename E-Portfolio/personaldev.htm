My Experiences

Role Date Description
Leader and Chairman of the Manchester Young Carers Group October 2017-Present I currently lead a dedicated group of young carers in Manchester. We have directly influenced the Manchester City Council's Young Carers Strategy with our Be Bothered Campaign. My aim as Chairman and Leader is to recruit more members, register as a charity, and become the independent, centralised young carers organisation in Manchester.
Executive Member of the Manchester Youth Council July 2017-Present I have been an executive member working with other young people in Manchester on youth issues such as A Curriculum for Life, Votes at 16 and Tackling Racism and Religious Discrimination. I have written agendas, chaired meetings, represented young people locally, regionally and nationally, sat in council chamber meetings, ran for Member of Youth Parliament, designed leaflets and other information, etc.
Steering Group Member of the Children's Rights Alliance for England November 2016-Present Joining in late November, I have been involved in the planning and delivery of our campaign "Change It!" which challenges the scandal of children and young people living in temporary accommodation, like B&B's, for over 6 weeks. We have recently lobbied Parliament on this, but the fight isn't over till real change happens.
National Participation Group Member of NSPCC January 2013-Present I have been actively involved with this wonderful charity for many years, and have made an impact locally in Manchester where they had a NSPCC centre, by which I ensured the place was welcoming for all children and young people. One of my proudest achievements is lobbying Parliament on Childline's 30th Birthday, highlighting the importance of keeping the service free and up and running.
